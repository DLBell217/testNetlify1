Rubik's Cube Solving: my story

My fascination with Rubik's Cubes started at an early age. I put countless hours into finding a way to solve them. And for a time, that was all I could do. I struggled and turmoiled, each turn of the cube seeming so good, but ultimately falling short.

After a while, I grew tired of feeling inept. I decided the internet would be where I made my final stand. Searching through the countless articles, I finally found hope. A simple website, the name of which is lost in the sands of time, gave me what I wanted. There were six steps to solving a cube, all I had to do was learn them.

It took a week to memorize the steps. At first, I struggled to solve the cube in under 10 minutes. Now, I can do it in under 1 minute. Years of practice and learning shortcuts has honed my skills to a comfortable position. A far cry from the best, but satisfactory for me.

Chess

Chess was a similarly life long endeaver. I played it with my father as long as I can remember. I didn't really start getting into it until around 2020, when I found the GothamChess YouTube channel.

Now, I am the "President" of the SUNY Oneonta Chess Club. We aren't officially recognized, but that was our choice. I play a whole lot on Chess.com. I also enjoy doing chess puzzles as frequently as I can.
